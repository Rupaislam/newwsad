The caretaker government of Pakistan’s Punjab province has declared an ‘environmental and health emergency’ in three districts including Lahore due to air pollution. This situation will continue for four days from tomorrow Thursday to Sunday to reduce pollution. A holiday has been declared in those three districts for four days. Dawn’s news.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

According to the announcement given by the provincial government, all the markets, shopping malls, restaurants, cinema halls, gymnasiums, schools and government and private offices of the three districts will be closed on those four days. Apart from Lahore, the announcement will be effective in Gojranwala and Hafizabad districts. Movement of government and private vehicles will also be limited in these three districts.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

According to the government notification, drug shops, petrol pumps, bakeries, grocery stores, dairy shops, vegetable and meat shops will remain open.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The caretaker chief minister of the province, Mohsin Naqvi, said that Section 144 has been imposed in these three districts. He said that next Thursday and Friday, public holidays have been announced in those three districts. And Saturday and Sunday are weekends.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Chief Minister said that the government will not close the factories. Pollution needs to be stopped in Lahore. The city environment needs rest.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Mohsin Naqvi said, ‘Children and elderly people are suffering from asthma and eye diseases.’<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Switzerland-based organization regularly highlights the air pollution situation of the world’s cities. This live or instant AQI index is based on air quality and informs and alerts people about how clean or polluted the air is in a particular city.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

According to IQAir data, Lahore has been at the top of the world for air pollution for almost two weeks. Today, Monday, the city is at the top position. The city has a score of 455.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

According to the IQAir criteria, a score of 51 to 100 is considered ‘moderate’ or ‘acceptable’ air quality. A score of 101 to 150 is considered ‘unhealthy for sensitive groups’. People in sensitive groups are most at risk when air pollution is high. Among them are the elderly, children, pregnant women, people suffering from complex diseases. Experts advise that special care should be taken about them.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

A score of 151 to 200 is ‘unhealthy’ air. A score of 201 to 300 is considered ‘very unhealthy’ air. A score of 301 and above is considered ‘disastrous’ or ‘risky’.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
